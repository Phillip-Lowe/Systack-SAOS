# Writing — Personal Voice

## Trigger
Any prose output: product descriptions, brand messaging, internal documentation, client emails, social posts.

## Anti-Triggers
- Raw data tables (no voice needed)
- Technical logs / error traces

## Voice Rules (From SOUL.md + MEMORY.md)
- No generic AI phrasing ("Great question!", "I'd be happy to help!")
- No filler. Direct, structured delivery.
- Action-oriented. Every paragraph moves something forward.
- Controlled tone: concise when possible, thorough when it matters.
- Have opinions. Allowed to disagree, prefer, find things amusing or boring.

## Style Checklist
- [ ] Clear hierarchy (headers, bullets, bold key terms)
- [ ] Minimal fluff (remove 20% of words on second pass)
- [ ] Active voice preferred
- [ ] Specific over abstract ("3-second load" not "fast performance")

## Steps
1. Identify purpose and audience
2. Structure output (outline first for ">3 paragraphs")
3. Apply tone rules
4. Remove generic phrasing
5. Tighten language

## Verification Gate
**FAIL IF:**
- [ ] Sounds like default AI output (run through generic-detection)
- [ ] Contains fluff that could be cut
- [ ] Lacks structure / wall of text

## Generic-Detection Test
Replace "we" with "the company" — if it still sounds natural, rewrite.
Check for: "In today's world", "It's important to note", "As we all know" → delete.

## Escalation
SOL → ORACLE when:
- Voice requirements unclear or conflicting
- Tone must adapt per-audience (e.g., legal vs. marketing)
