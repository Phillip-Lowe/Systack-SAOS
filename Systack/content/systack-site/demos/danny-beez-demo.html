From Nassau to Little Rock

Danielle Johnson — better known as Danny Beez — was born and raised in The Bahamas with deep roots in Exuma and Nassau. After losing her grandmother, she turned grief into purpose, honoring the woman who first taught her to cook.

"It all started with the love for my grandma. Every time I cook, it's like I feel like she's right there on my shoulder."

When COVID shut down the hospitality industry, Danny packed two duffel bags, saved $5,000, and moved to Little Rock. She hustled. She worked. And she built a food truck with her own two hands.

"I saved five thousand dollars, packed my whole life up into two duffel bags, took that leap. I bought me a car. Had two thousand left, and then I had to go hit the streets. Working, working, working, hustling."

Then one video changed everything — a jerk chicken quesadilla went viral on TikTok. Now Danny's phone rings all day. She's the first Bahamian catering company and food truck in Arkansas, and she's putting The Bahamas on the map one plate at a time.

"I want people to know that The Bahamas is here. We're not only a beautiful vacation spot — we're deep-rooted, and we actually have talented, passionate individuals over here."

🇧🇸 Bahamian flag on the truck. Passion on every plate. This is Danny Beez.
