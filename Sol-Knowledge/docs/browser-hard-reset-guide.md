# Browser Hard Reset / Hard Refresh Guide (Mac)

> **Quick Reference:** Use this when a webpage is glitchy, not loading correctly, or you suspect cached data is causing issues.

---

## Method 1: Hard Refresh a Single Webpage

Bypass the local cache to fetch the freshest data from the server.

### Google Chrome, Brave, Firefox, or Microsoft Edge

| Method | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Keyboard Shortcut** | Press **⌘ + Shift + R** |
| **Alternative** | Hold **Shift** and click the Reload icon in the top menu bar |
| **Deepest Clear** | Press **⌘ + Option + I** to open Developer Tools. Right-click the Reload icon and select **Empty Cache and Hard Reload** |

### Apple Safari

| Method | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Quick Shortcut** | Press **⌘ + Option + R** |
| **Manual Clear** | Press **⌘ + Option + E** to empty cache background files, then press **⌘ + R** to reload |

---

## Method 2: Total Factory Reset (Wipe All Data)

⚠️ **Warning:** This will sign you out of accounts and reset your homepages.

### Google Chrome Total Reset

1. Open Google Chrome
2. Click the three vertical dots (⋮) in the upper-right corner and select **Settings**
3. On the left sidebar, click **Reset settings**
4. Click **Restore settings to their original defaults**
5. Confirm by clicking **Reset settings**

### Apple Safari Total Reset

Modern macOS does not have a single "Reset" button for Safari, but you can clear everything manually:

1. Open Safari
2. Click **Safari** in the top Mac menu bar and choose **Clear History...**
3. Change the dropdown to **all history** and click **Clear History**
4. Go back to the top menu, choose **Settings** (or **Preferences**), and select the **Privacy** tab
5. Click **Manage Website Data...**, then click **Remove All** to delete tracking cookies
6. Click the **Extensions** tab and uninstall any unrecognized extensions

---

## When to Use Each Method

| Situation | Recommended Action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Single page not loading correctly | Method 1: Hard Refresh |
| Browser constantly freezing or crashing | Method 2: Factory Reset |
| Suspected adware or redirect virus | Method 2: Factory Reset + check extensions |
| Website showing old content after update | Method 1: Hard Refresh |
| Login issues on a specific site | Method 1: Hard Refresh, then check cookies |
